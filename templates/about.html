Background

In Kyrgyzstan, I studied jurisprudence and established my career in the law enforcement, Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Kyrgyz Republic mvd from 2004 - 2010. I gained experience in various departments such as Organized Crime Unit, Drug Enforcement deparment, Criminal Investigation and in Social Communication department of the Police organization. When I became officer, district inspector, rank lieutenant, in a certain area with a population of more than 3000 inhabitants in the southern city Osh, I had the following responsibilities:
Communication with representatives of communities and individuals Data collection on incoming and outgoing residents as well as on suspects, and persecuted felons Paperwork: receiving of appeals, complaints, statements Investigate and resolve offences
Within five years in the Police Organisation, I received several awards for solving crimes. Teamwork and solving people's problems has been my passion at work, which I am proud of for my well-deserved service in the law enforcement agencies in Kyrgyzstan.

The day Ground Zero

The unexpected in life can turn a persons whole life and plans upside down

On June 10, 2010, in Kyrgyzstan an inter-ethnic conflict found its place. The south of the country was engulfed in chaos and lawlessness, and the Government of Kyrgyzstan had not prevented this conflict although all knew of that. Then, while working in Criminal investigation Unit, also knew that people were preparing for that conflict in advance. For failure to take action in a timely manner, the Government of Kyrgyzstan had to ask for military assistance from other countries on June 12 - of no effect. During the conflict, I had to save the lives of the Uzbeks, helped them get out of the conflict zone to safe places in exercising of my duty. When the conflict ends, the government and the State Committee for National Security GKNB begins to consider people who helped the Uzbeks as enemies of the country, as a result of which I, among others, became a victim of persecution for the assistance provided. Since that time, I have been staying abroad.

A Friend in Need is a Friend Indeed!

Switzerland/ Swiss

While trying to get to Sweden from Russia, I was brought to Switzerland instead where I had to apply for political asylum. From November 2010 to November 2014, I was in Switzerland as a refugee waiting for a decision. Meanwhile I learned German language, played basketball for the local Giants-3 team and got acquainted with traditions and faith of Swiss folk. As an old adage goes, a stranger is just a friend you do not know , there I met a friend, Leander who you can rely on, have no doubts and be confident in him, no matter what others say. But alas, in 2014 it turned out that the Switzerland and the government of Kyrgyzstan are in close relations in the trade of gold extracted in Kyrgyzstan, that the Swiss Migration Service with vague reasons refused to grant me political asylum from threats and unfounded persecution, that this decision has not even been reviewed by a Swiss court for correctness and legality. And I had to leave Switzerland in search of a safe country.


Ecuador

It turned out so that life carried me after Switzerland across the Atlantic Ocean and I landed in Ecuador according to the answer of the Ecuadorian Embassy in Swiss. From 2015 to 2018, I have been staying in Ecuador, the land of adventurers and rascals. In this country I have seen and discovered much that I barely could even imagine in my mind from what I have seen. A country that is rapidly developing in words and papers, but not in deeds. I have to admit that it was hard for me there, as the German saying goes: some flee a stream and fall into the Rhine. I often had to fight for my rights and life, especially with the employees of the organisation which represented the rights of refugees in Ecuador. Without even waiting for an answer to my application for political asylum, I wanted, in March 2016, to leave Ecuador for a safer country, but the Ecuadorian officials did not let me on the plane even after having seen a written reply from the Panamanian embassy in hand. This incident, led me to meet a local family who offered me a helping hand from what they had heard and seen happened to me there. I saw thereby the other side of the country where people still have hope and faith in justice. Thanks to that family, Lourdes, I could unhindered leave from Ecuador for Europe in September 2018 by plane. I hope to meet my closest Ecuadorian friends in Europe to thank them for their kindness.
